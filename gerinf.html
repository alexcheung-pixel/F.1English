📘 To‑Infinitives

We use to + base form of the verb after these verbs:

afford負擔得起expect期望love喜愛pretend假裝
agree同意fail失敗manage設法做到promise承諾
ask詢問hope希望need需要refuse拒絕
choose選擇forget忘記plan計畫remember記得
decide決定learn學習prepare準備want想要
  • John wants to eat dinner now.
  • She decided to study for the exam tonight.
  • They hope to win the competition tomorrow.

Rule: Use to + base form after these verbs.

⚙️ Bare Infinitives

We use the base form of the verb after these verbs and modals:

cancouldmaymightmust
shallshouldwillwouldmake/let
  • I can swim very fast.
  • You should study every day.
  • They let him go home early.

Rule: Do not add “to” after these verbs or modals.

🎨 Gerunds (V‑ing)

Rule 1 – When a Verb is the Subject

  • Swimming is a good exercise.
  • Reading improves your vocabulary.
  • Playing football keeps you healthy.

Rule 2 – After Certain Verbs

like喜歡love熱愛dislike不喜歡hate討厭prefer偏好
avoid避免finish完成keep保持mind介意practise練習
stop停止suggest建議consider考慮enjoy享受imagine想像
mention提及miss想念recommend推薦
  • She enjoys reading novels before bed.
  • He stopped smoking last year.
  • We finished doing our homework early.

Rule 3 – After Prepositions

Use a gerund after each of these prepositions:

aboutafterbeforeinon
ofwithbyforfrom
  • She is interested in learning Spanish.
  • They talked about going abroad for vacation.
  • He got tired of waiting for the bus.
